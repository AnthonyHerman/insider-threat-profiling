## Interpretation

- Naive automated agents are separated from humans at **AUC 1.000** (accuracy 1.000).
- A perfect behavioural mimic (`e=1`) collapses detection toward chance (**AUC 0.500**), as theory predicts: an adversary that perfectly reproduces human timing *is*, behaviourally, indistinguishable. The value of the detector is the **cost** it imposes to get there.
- Detection degrades monotonically with evasion effort; the evasion-robust (Tier-2/3) features keep AUC elevated through the mid-budget range where cheap (Tier-1) mimicry alone would already have collapsed it.

## Limitations

These results are **synthetic**: sessions are sampled from documented behavioural distributions, not collected from instrumented humans and agents in the field. They validate that the pipeline and model separate the *modelled* behaviours and quantify the evasion trade-off; they are **not** a claim of field accuracy. A field study with IRB-approved data collection is future work.
